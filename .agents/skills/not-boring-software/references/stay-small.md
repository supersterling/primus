## Stay Small on Purpose

Scaling the team does not produce better work. For differentiation-driven design, a small team is a feature — not a limitation to overcome.

> "We've stayed very small because we've found that we can't really do better work by scaling."
> — Andy Allen

### Why small works for this kind of product

- **Fewer opinions means stronger point of view.** The more people involved in decisions, the more the product gets averaged out toward the middle. A strong aesthetic voice requires a small number of people who share it.
- **Speed of creative iteration.** A two-person team can decide, build, and test in the time a larger team spends in a kickoff meeting.
- **No organizational drag.** No process to maintain, no alignment meetings, no design system debates. The work is the work.
- **Lower "enough" threshold.** A small team needs less revenue to be sustainable. This removes the pressure to scale the product in ways that would compromise it.

### What this requires

Staying small only works if:

- The team is exceptionally capable (Andy describes his technical partner as "an engineering marvel and a graphics genius")
- The scope is defined by what a small team can do *well*, not what would be possible with more people
- Revenue expectations match the structure — not VC-scale returns, but sustainable for the people involved

### The VC trap

Raising money changes the math. Once you've taken $45M, you need a $45M+ outcome. A product that would have been a great small business becomes a failed startup. The product gets pushed toward growth levers — more features, broader appeal, faster shipping — at the cost of the very thing that made it interesting.

> "I think the business we had created was a valid business, but it was not a VC unicorn business. If someone had told me how it would end, I'd have said: sign me up. But it just wasn't a VC business."
> — Andy Allen

### Rule

**Design your team size around the kind of work you want to do, not the kind of growth you think you're supposed to want.**

If the work requires a strong point of view, a tiny team is an advantage. Protect it.
