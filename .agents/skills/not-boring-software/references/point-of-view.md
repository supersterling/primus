## Software with a Point of View

The goal is software where you can see the creator in the work. Not branded. Not personalized. *Authored* — the way a film, a piece of furniture, or a fashion collection is authored. There's a perspective behind it, and you can feel it.

> "This idea that you, as a designer, can have a strong point of view that you articulate through software — that other people can see you, the creator, in the work you put out."
> — Andy Allen

### What this means in practice

A product with a point of view has opinions about:

- **What should be easy** — and what should require intention
- **What should feel serious** — and what should feel playful
- **What to include** — and what to leave out entirely
- **How it should sound, move, and respond** — not just what it should display

These opinions are consistent. They accumulate. By the time a user has spent 10 minutes with the product, they understand its personality — even if they couldn't articulate it.

### What it rules out

- Adding a feature because users asked for it, if it conflicts with the product's voice
- Designing by committee or A/B testing your way to a personality
- Following platform conventions when they clash with what the product is trying to say
- Shipping something that "works" but feels like it was designed by no one

### Taste

Taste is not a fixed gift — it's a perspective that gets exercised. Most designers have it in them. They just haven't had the space to develop it.

> "A lot of people have it in them. They have some unique perspective. They just haven't had the opportunity to nurture that or express it."
> — Andy Allen

The constraint of indie, bootstrapped work is actually an advantage here: no one is asking you to sand down your opinions. The product can be exactly as weird, specific, or opinionated as you want it to be. That specificity *is* the product.

### Rule

**Make something where someone could pick it up, use it for five minutes, and have a sense of who made it.** Not from branding. From the decisions themselves — what it does, how it responds, what it refuses to be.

If your product could have been made by anyone, it doesn't have a point of view yet.
